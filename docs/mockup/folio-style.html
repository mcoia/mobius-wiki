MOBIUS

This documentation supports Missouri's premier library consortium. MOBIUS provides shared resources and services to 72 member libraries across Missouri and neighboring states.

This section of the documentation contains links to external sites. Please be advised that these external sites are not maintained by MOBIUS and may be aligned with different MOBIUS releases or services.

Welcome to MOBIUS documentation. MOBIUS is a collaborative library consortium that provides access to shared collections, innovative technologies, and professional expertise. This open platform moves the library industry beyond traditional library management systems, offering libraries more choices of features and services.

A modern library ecosystem

The MOBIUS consortium aims to facilitate a sustainable, community-driven collaboration around the creation of modern technology infrastructure that empowers libraries through shared applications to manage library resources and expand library value.

Community collaboration

Development of MOBIUS services is driven by the transparent collaboration of librarians, developers, and vendors. A governance structure provides oversight of consortium development, with representation from the many member institutions and organizations involved. A healthy number of communication channels supports ongoing interactions to move the consortium forward.

History and Organization

MOBIUS was established in July 1998 by 50 libraries representing Missouri colleges and universities, with the University of Missouri acting as the initial host institution. The organization's headquarters remain in Columbia, Missouri, where a dedicated staff provides implementation support, training, technical assistance, and ongoing system management for member libraries.

Membership and Scope

MOBIUS membership has grown significantly beyond its Missouri origins. Today, the consortium includes libraries from Oklahoma, Iowa, Kansas, and Texas, representing:

  • 66 academic libraries – Universities, colleges, and research institutions
  • 7 public libraries – Serving communities across multiple states
  • 3 special libraries – Specialized collections and resources
  • Missouri State Library – State-level coordination and support

Technology Infrastructure

FOLIO Library Services Platform

MOBIUS has adopted the FOLIO Library Services Platform, hosted by EBSCO Information Services. FOLIO is an open-source, next-generation library management system that replaces traditional integrated library systems (ILS). The platform uses a microservices architecture, allowing libraries to customize functionality through modular apps that cover:

  • Circulation management – Check-in/check-out, holds, and renewals
  • Cataloging and metadata – MARC records and bibliographic control
  • Acquisitions and invoicing – Purchase orders and budget management
  • Electronic resource management – Licenses and access control

The University of Missouri System completed its migration to FOLIO in 2022, with EBSCO providing comprehensive implementation, hosting, and support services. MOBIUS operates FOLIO as a multi-tenant shared system, enabling member libraries to benefit from collective resources while maintaining individual institutional configurations.

OpenRS Resource Sharing

As a founding member of the Open Resource Sharing Coalition, MOBIUS became the first consortium to implement OpenRS in May 2024. OpenRS is an innovative open-source resource sharing platform developed in partnership with the Open Library Foundation, EBSCO Information Services, and Knowledge Integration.

OpenRS addresses critical gaps in consortial resource sharing by providing:

  • Unmediated patron requesting – Library users can directly request materials from other consortium members
  • ILS-agnostic integration – Works with multiple library systems including FOLIO and Sierra
  • Multi-type consortium support – Accommodates academic, public, and special libraries within a single framework
  • Containerized architecture – Flexible deployment options for local or hosted implementations

Services and Benefits

MOBIUS provides member libraries with comprehensive services designed to maximize resource access and operational efficiency:

  • Resource sharing – Direct borrowing and lending among member libraries through the union catalog
  • Courier service – Cost-effective physical delivery of materials between institutions
  • Professional development – Training opportunities, annual conferences, and networking events
  • Electronic resources – Cooperative purchasing and licensing of online databases and e-content
  • Technical support – Help desk services, implementation assistance, and system maintenance
  • OER partnerships – Support for open educational resources to enhance student success

Governance and Mission

MOBIUS operates under a set of bylaws and articles of incorporation that formalize its purpose, membership guidelines, and organizational structure. The consortium is governed by a Board of Directors and led by Executive Director Donna Bacon.

The organization's core values emphasize:

  • Fostering a culture of collaboration and community
  • Supporting cooperative collection development
  • Adopting technologies that enhance user experience
  • Practicing fiscally responsible stewardship
  • Engaging all member libraries and respecting diversity
